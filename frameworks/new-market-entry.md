# New Market Entry Framework

## What This Question Tests
Tests your ability to evaluate whether a company should enter a new market — using competitive analysis, capability assessment, and opportunity sizing.

## Evaluation Criteria
- Is your analysis clearly structured and logical?
- Can you synthesize your analysis into an objective recommendation?
- Can you back up your decision with market size estimates?
- Do you consider competitive advantage and capabilities, not just market attractiveness?

---

## The Framework (5 Steps)

### Step 1: Where to Play
Identify where in the new industry's value chain the company could compete:
- What parts of the value chain align with the company's mission?
- What product synergies exist between the company's current business and the new market?
- Upstream (infrastructure/supply)? Midstream (platform)? Downstream (consumer-facing)?

### Step 2: How to Win
Run a competitive and customer analysis:
- Who are the incumbents? What are their strengths and weaknesses?
- Can the entering company compete given its current capabilities?
- What is the sustainable competitive advantage in this market? (network effects, data, distribution, brand, cost structure)
- Would the company have a differentiated position, or would it be "me too"?

### Step 3: Core Capabilities
What capabilities are required to win in this market?
- Technology
- Market / customer knowledge
- Sales channels and distribution
- Partnerships / supplier relationships
- Regulatory/compliance expertise

Assess: Does the company have them? Can it acquire them (build, buy, partner)?

### Step 4: Size of Opportunity
Estimate the market size:
- Total Addressable Market (TAM)
- Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM) — realistic portion the company could capture
- Is this large enough to be strategically meaningful?
- What's the timeline to profitability?

(Use the Estimation framework to size the market if needed)

### Step 5: Recommendation
Synthesize your analysis into a clear recommendation:
- Should the company enter? Why/why not?
- If yes: how (build, buy, partner)? Which segment to target first?
- What are the key risks and how would you mitigate them?

---

## Common Question Examples
- "Should Facebook enter the travel industry?"
- "Should Google launch a healthcare product?"
- "Should Uber enter the grocery delivery market?"
- "Should [company] expand to [new country/vertical]?"

## Coaching Tips
- "Where to Play" is about alignment with mission and synergies — don't skip this
- Competitive advantage matters more than market size — a huge market is worthless if you can't win
- Always size the market — even a rough estimate shows analytical rigor
- The recommendation should flow logically from the analysis — no surprises
