# Growth Strategy Framework

## What This Question Tests
Tests your ability to think strategically about growing a product's revenue and market share given competitive dynamics, market trends, and business context.

## Evaluation Criteria
- Knowledgeable about growth strategies?
- Can you think critically about which strategy fits *this* product and market?
- Structured and organized in your answer?
- Do you have original ideas?
- Articulate delivery?

---

## The Framework

### Step 1: Competitive Landscape
- What are the product's strengths and weaknesses relative to competitors?
- Who are the market leaders?
- How does the product compare on key dimensions (price, UX, features, distribution)?

### Step 2: Market & Technology Drivers
- What are the macro trends shaping this market?
- Are there technology shifts creating new growth opportunities or threats?
- Where is the market in its lifecycle (emerging / growing / mature / declining)?

### Step 3: Growth Strategies
Based on your competitive and market analysis, evaluate which strategies apply:

| Strategy | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Product extension** | Add adjacent products/features to expand wallet share |
| **Strengthen competitive position** | Double down on what already differentiates |
| **Geographic expansion** | Enter new regions or countries |
| **New market entry** | Target new customer segments or verticals |
| **Partnerships / distribution** | Leverage partner channels to reach new users |
| **Acquisition** | Buy a competitor or complementary product |
| **Pricing strategy** | Use pricing to compete for market share |
| **Platform/ecosystem** | Create a platform effect to lock in users/developers |

### Step 4: Summarize & Recommend
- Summarize the best 2–3 growth opportunities
- Recommend the one(s) with the highest impact relative to effort and risk
- Explain why you're deprioritizing other options

---

## Example Question Analysis
**"Why did Amazon buy Zappos/Diapers.com but keep them independent?"**

Framework: Company mission → Customer perception → Competitive threat → Strategy rationale → Assessment

Key insight: Amazon's mission is to be the one-stop shop. Best-in-class specialty retailers are a threat because customers perceive them as having better selection/expertise in their niche. Buying and keeping them independent retains brand equity while eliminating the competitive threat — and Amazon's fulfillment improves the customer experience.

## Common Question Examples
- "How would you grow [product] by 20% in the next year?"
- "Uber's growth is flat in its core market. What would you do?"
- "How would you grow Spotify's podcast listening?"
- "What growth strategies would you recommend for [company]?"

## Coaching Tips
- Ground your strategies in the competitive and market analysis — don't jump to tactics
- The best growth strategies leverage existing strengths rather than trying to build new ones from scratch
- Consider second-order effects (e.g., entering a new market might cannibalize existing revenue)
- Always end with a clear prioritized recommendation
