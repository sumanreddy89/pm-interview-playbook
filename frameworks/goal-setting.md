# Goal Setting Framework

## What This Question Tests
Tests your ability to define goals for a product team in a top-down, business-aligned way — from business objective down to product goal.

## Evaluation Criteria
- Do you identify a business objective and explain why it matters?
- Do you identify the key users to focus on?
- Do you identify and prioritize user outcomes to achieve?
- Do you clearly articulate how solving user outcomes impacts the business objective?
- Clear and succinct communication?

---

## The Framework

### Part 1: Business Objective

#### Step 1: Context
Describe the product and the user problem it solves. Mention company strategy if you know it.

#### Step 2: State of Product
Assess where the product is in its lifecycle:
- Not yet launched / just launched / growing / mature
- How is it performing relative to competitors?
- Is it growing or plateauing?

#### Step 3: Define the Business Goal
Based on the product state, company strategy, market, and your POV, identify the right business goal to prioritize. Examples:
- Increase revenue
- Reduce costs
- Increase retention
- Expand into new markets
- Grow market share

---

### Part 2: Product Goal

#### Step 4: Users
Identify user segments. Prioritize the segment(s) most likely to impact the business goal.

#### Step 5: Challenges / Gaps
For the prioritized segment, identify the most significant challenges or product gaps they experience. Pick the challenge that, if solved, would most impact the business goal.

#### Step 6: Reframe as a Product Goal
Reframe the challenge as an **aspirational user outcome** — a destination, not a directive. The goal should tell the team *where* to go, not *what* to build.

> ❌ "Build an AI writing assistant" (a directive)
> ✅ "Help professionals compose letters and documents with confidence" (an outcome)

---

## Example
**Question:** "You are the PM of Grammarly. What goal would you set?"

- **Context**: Grammarly is the #1 grammar tool; strong partnerships with Google Docs
- **State of Product**: Market leader, growing; AI capabilities are advancing rapidly
- **Business Goal**: Develop advanced AI features to grow market share
- **Segment**: Professionals who write frequently (broadest reach)
- **Key Gap**: No tool helps compose entire letters/documents (not just corrections)
- **Product Goal**: "Help people write entire letters and documents" — broader reach than marketing copy or document summarization

---

## Common Question Examples
- "You are the PM of [product]. What goal would you set?"
- "How would you define success for [product]?"
- "What OKRs would you set for [product team]?"

## Coaching Tips
- The top-down flow (business → product) signals strategic thinking
- The product goal should be a *destination*, not a feature spec
- Always explain *why* you prioritized one segment/challenge over others — the reasoning matters more than the choice
