# Marketing Plan Framework

## What This Question Tests
Tests your knowledge of what makes an effective marketing plan — goal-first thinking, competitive awareness, and tactical creativity.

## Evaluation Criteria
- Do you start with a marketing goal, or jump straight to listing activities?
- Do you analyze the company, product, competition, and customers before recommending tactics?
- Do you have original, specific marketing ideas?
- Articulate and structured delivery?

---

## The Framework

### Step 1: State (or Ask for) the Marketing Goal
Before planning any activities, align on the goal. Examples:
- Acquire users in a new segment
- Increase brand awareness in a new market
- Drive trial of a new feature
- Re-engage churned users
- Defend market share from a new competitor

### Step 2: Analyze (4Cs)
Ground your plan in analysis before jumping to tactics:

**Company**: What are the company's goals, strengths, and weaknesses?
**Product**: What does it do? What's the value proposition? What makes it differentiated?
**Competition**: Who are they? How are they positioned? Where are their gaps?
**Customers**: Who are they? What do they want? How do they currently buy? What channels do they use?
**Market Landscape**: Trends, seasonality, regulatory issues, geography?

### Step 3: Define/Restate the Marketing Goal
Based on your analysis, confirm or refine the marketing goal.

### Step 4: Marketing Activities
Organize activities by type:

**Product**: Any feature changes needed to support the marketing goal? (e.g., adding a free trial tier)

**Distribution / Channel**:
- Direct: website, in-app
- Indirect: App Store, retailers, resellers, partnerships

**Promotion**:
- *Inbound*: SEO, content marketing, social media, community, referral programs
- *Outbound*: paid ads (search, social, display), PR, influencer, email, events, trade shows, co-marketing

**Pricing**: Does pricing need to change to support the goal? (e.g., promotional pricing, bundling)

### Step 5: Metrics
How will you measure success? Connect each activity to a measurable outcome (CTR, CAC, conversion rate, brand awareness lift, etc.)

---

## Common Question Examples
- "Design a marketing plan for [new product] launch."
- "How would you market [feature] to enterprise customers?"
- "We're launching in [new country]. How do you approach marketing?"
- "How would you re-engage churned Spotify users?"

## Coaching Tips
- Goal-first is the biggest differentiator — interviewers can tell when candidates skip straight to tactics
- The 4C analysis should be brief but show you've thought about the context
- Mix inbound and outbound — relying on only one shows limited marketing range
- The best answers connect tactics directly back to the goal and the customer insight
