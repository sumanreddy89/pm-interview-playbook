# Favorite Product Framework

## What This Question Tests
Whether you analyze products regularly, have personal insights on design, and can connect product features to user delight. Tests product intuition.

## Evaluation Criteria
- Can you structure your answer logically?
- Can you think *deeply* about the product — connect features to why they delight users?
- Are you knowledgeable about competitive alternatives?
- Are you exhaustive about types of users and use cases?
- Are you articulate, not rambling?

---

## The Framework

### 1. Pick the Right Product
Choose something you love and use frequently. Personal connection = genuine insight + visible passion.

### 2. Describe the Product
- What does it do?
- Who are the users, and what do they want to accomplish?
- If you're a user, why do you use it?

### 3. How Does It Delight Users?
Name **three things** the product does that delight users:
- What problems is it solving?
- What is the emotional job-to-be-done?
- Is there anything about the business model that benefits users?

### 4. Competitive Comparison
- What are the main alternatives?
- Why are those alternatives not preferred?
- What does this product do that competitors can't or don't?

### 5. How Would You Improve It? (Optional)
- Which metric could be improved (retention, engagement, acquisition, revenue)?
- What use case scenarios support your improvement ideas?
- What would you change or add to address those use cases?

## Example Flow
> "My favorite product is Bitmoji. It's a personalized emoji keyboard that lets you create a caricature avatar of yourself. Users are anyone who communicates via messaging apps — the core job-to-be-done is expressing emotion and personality beyond what text can convey. What delights users is: (1) the humor of seeing yourself in funny scenarios, (2) the ability to express nuanced feelings, (3) personalization of the avatar. Competitors like generic emoji packs and GIFs don't show *you*, so they're less personal and funny. To improve it, I'd add group bitmojis and branded avatar clothing to drive engagement and ad revenue..."

## Common Question Examples
- "What is your favorite product and why?"
- "Tell me about a product you think is well-designed."
- "What's an app you use every day and why?"
- "What product would you be most excited to work on and why?"

## Coaching Tips
- Don't just describe what you *like* — explain *why* the design choices produce that response in users
- The competitive comparison shows you think about the market, not just the product in isolation
- The improvement section is optional but separates good answers from great ones — shows product intuition beyond appreciation
- Avoid obvious choices (iPhone, Google Maps) unless you have genuinely fresh insights about them
