# Product Launch Framework

## What This Question Tests
Tests whether you can think strategically about a product launch — with goal-setting, analysis, and phased planning covering pre-, during-, and post-launch.

## Evaluation Criteria
- Do you have a basic understanding of business and implementation issues for a product launch?
- Do you start with launch goals before planning activities?
- Do you analyze key business factors before designing the launch?
- Do you provide detailed, specific launch activities with examples?

---

## The Framework

### Step 1: Clarify
Ask clarifying questions if anything is ambiguous:
- What type of product is this? (consumer vs. enterprise, app vs. hardware, etc.)
- What market(s) is the launch targeting?
- Are there specific constraints (budget, timeline, geography)?

### Step 2: Analysis
Analyze before designing the launch plan:

**Product**: What does it do? What problem does it solve for users?
**Customers**: Who are they? What do they want? How do they discover products?
**Competition**: Who are the main competitors? What are their strengths/weaknesses?
**Environment**: Regulations, market trends, or seasonal factors that affect the launch?

### Step 3: Launch Goal
State the launch goal clearly. Common goals:
- **Validate product-market fit**: Learn whether the product resonates before scaling
- **Maximize growth**: Acquire as many users as fast as possible
- **Drive revenue**: Optimize for profitable users early
- **Generate buzz**: Create media coverage and word of mouth

The goal determines everything else in the plan.

### Step 4: Strategy Decisions
Make explicit choices:
- **Market**: Which geography/segment to launch in first?
- **Growth control**: Invite-only (controlled) vs. open launch (maximum reach)?
- **Product scope**: MVP vs. full product? What's in/out of v1?
- **Risk areas**: What could go wrong? How do you mitigate?

### Step 5: Implementation Plan

#### Pre-Launch
- **Marketing**: Inbound (content, SEO, social, community) + Outbound (PR, ads, events, trade shows)
- **Partnerships**: Co-branding, co-sales, distribution partnerships
- **Beta/early access**: Identify and recruit early adopters
- **Internal readiness**: Customer support trained, monitoring in place, rollback plan ready

#### During Launch
- **Marketing**: Sustained inbound + outbound campaign
- **Distribution**: Which channels? (app stores, website, retail, enterprise sales)
- **Partnerships**: Co-announcements, joint promotions
- **Pricing**: Launch pricing strategy (penetration, value-based, promotional?)
- **Real-time monitoring**: Track key metrics daily; rapid response to issues

#### Post-Launch
- **Success metrics**: How will you measure launch success? (user growth, revenue, NPS, press mentions)
- **Feedback loop**: How are you collecting user feedback? (support tickets, reviews, user interviews)
- **Iteration**: What's the plan for the first post-launch update?
- **Expansion**: What markets/features are next if the launch succeeds?

## Common Question Examples
- "How would you launch [new product/feature]?"
- "Walk me through how you'd launch a new enterprise product."
- "You're launching in a new country. What's your plan?"
- "How do you launch a product when you're a small startup with no marketing budget?"

## Coaching Tips
- Launch goal drives everything — without it, your plan has no coherence
- The analysis section should be brief but show you've thought about the user and the competition
- Pre/during/post structure is the clearest way to organize launch activities — don't just list tactics randomly
- Post-launch is where many candidates have weak answers — show you're thinking about sustained success, not just day one
