# Pricing Strategy Framework

## What This Question Tests
Tests your knowledge of pricing strategy and how to apply it — considering business goals, market dynamics, differentiation, and competitive positioning.

## Evaluation Criteria
- Do you analyze key business parameters before naming a price/strategy?
- Can you formulate a pricing strategy supported by your analysis?
- Articulate and structured delivery?

---

## Key Parameters to Analyze First

Before naming a price or strategy, work through these dimensions:

| Parameter | Why It Matters |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Business goal** | Lower price → market share; Higher price → profit margin |
| **Differentiation** | Highly differentiated products can command premium pricing |
| **Market evolution** | Emerging (customers forming opinions) vs. Growing vs. Mature (cost competition) vs. Declining |
| **Cost structure** | Your minimum viable price floor |
| **Customer segments** | Different segments have different willingness to pay |
| **Competition** | What are competitors charging? What's the market reference price? |
| **Elasticity** | How sensitive are customers to price changes in this market? |

## Pricing Strategies

### Offensive Strategies (gaining market share)
- **Penetration pricing**: Launch below market price to quickly acquire users; raise later
- **Price leadership**: Be the lowest-cost option to dominate market share
- **Freemium**: Free basic tier to drive adoption; monetize power users

### Defensive Strategies (protecting market position)
- **Value-based pricing**: Price based on the value delivered to customers, not cost
- **Premium pricing**: Signal quality/exclusivity; use for highly differentiated products
- **Skimming**: Launch high, lower price over time as market matures and competition increases

### Market-Sensitive Strategies
- **Bundle pricing**: Package features/products together to increase perceived value
- **Dynamic pricing**: Vary price based on demand, time, or customer segment (e.g., Uber surge)
- **Tiered pricing**: Different price points for different levels of usage/features

---

## Putting It Together
1. Define the business goal (gain share vs. maximize profit?)
2. Assess differentiation — how much pricing power do you have?
3. Assess the market lifecycle — what stage constrains or enables your strategy?
4. Look at competitor pricing — what is the market reference price?
5. Identify your cost floor — the minimum you can charge sustainably
6. Choose a strategy that fits all of the above
7. Define metrics to evaluate the pricing strategy's success

---

## Common Question Examples
- "How would you price [new product]?"
- "Netflix is considering raising prices. How would you approach the decision?"
- "How would you price a new enterprise software product?"
- "Should [company] use a freemium or subscription model?"

## Coaching Tips
- Never name a price without first analyzing the parameters — doing so signals shallow thinking
- Value-based pricing is almost always the right answer for differentiated products
- Penetration pricing makes sense when network effects matter — getting to scale fast has compounding value
- Pricing has second-order effects: price communicates brand positioning, not just economics
