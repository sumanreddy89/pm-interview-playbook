# Brainstorming / Ideation Framework

## What This Question Tests
Tests divergent thinking and creativity. Interviewers want to see if you can generate novel, relevant ideas under pressure — not just incremental "more of the same" thinking.

## Evaluation Criteria
- Are you creative? Can you think outside the box?
- Do you generate multiple, differentiated ideas?
- Can you handle creative pressure without giving up?
- Are you articulate in presenting your ideas?

## The SCAMPER Technique
Use product/service attributes as a springboard, then apply SCAMPER to generate ideas:

| Letter | Action | What to Ask |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **S** | Substitute | What can be replaced? |
| **C** | Combine | What can be combined with something else? |
| **A** | Adapt | What can be adapted from another context? |
| **M** | Modify / Magnify | What can be changed, enlarged, or reduced? |
| **P** | Put to another use | How else could it be used? |
| **E** | Eliminate | What can be removed or simplified? |
| **R** | Reverse / Rearrange | What if it worked backwards? What if you rearranged components? |

## Brainstorming Framework
1. **Describe the product/service** — list its key attributes (materials, shape, size, use, cost, audience)
2. **Generate ideas via SCAMPER** — use each letter and the product attributes as triggers for associations
3. **Recommend the best idea** (optional) — apply criteria like feasibility, demand, cost, novelty to pick a favorite

## How to Present Your Answer
- Take a moment to write notes before speaking
- Work through SCAMPER associations out loud — narrate your thinking
- Group related ideas by the attribute they stem from
- End by recommending your favorite idea with a clear rationale

## Common Question Examples
- "What are alternative uses for a paperclip/chopstick/brick?"
- "Brainstorm new features for [product]."
- "How would you make [existing product] appeal to a new audience?"
- "Come up with 10 ideas for a new product in [space]."

## Coaching Tips
- Breadth matters: generate many ideas across multiple SCAMPER dimensions before filtering
- Creativity is a skill — practice by applying SCAMPER to a product every day
- The best answers show *unexpected* connections, not just obvious extensions
- Quantity first, then quality — don't self-censor during ideation
