# Product Improvement Framework

## What This Question Tests
Tests your analytical ability to identify and prioritize product improvements that drive business impact — not just nice-to-haves.

## Evaluation Criteria
- Do you ask clarifying questions to narrow scope?
- Do you frame improvements in the context of a business goal?
- Do you identify the right audience and the right problems?
- Are your solutions creative and specific?
- Are you aware of risks?
- Structured, logical, and succinct?

---

## The Framework (7 Steps)

### Step 1: Clarify
Ask clarifying questions to narrow scope before diving in:
- Is this for mobile or desktop?
- What's the timeline for the improvement?
- Are there budget or resource constraints?
- Which user segment should I focus on?

### Step 2: Business Goal
Paint the business picture to arrive at a goal:
- **Product**: What does it do? What problem does it solve? What's the revenue model?
- **Market**: How is it doing vs. competitors? Market trends? Technology shifts?
- **Business goal**: Based on the above, what should the company prioritize? (Improve acquisition, retention, engagement, monetization, or reduce costs?)

### Step 3: Segment
Identify user segments and prioritize the one most likely to impact the business goal:
- Who uses this product?
- Which segment has the most unmet needs relevant to the business goal?
- Which segment, if delighted, would have the highest business impact?

### Step 4: Pain Points
For the prioritized segment, identify 3–5 pain points. Prioritize the one most worth solving:
- Which pain is most frequent and severe?
- Which pain, if solved, would most directly impact the business goal?

### Step 5: Improvement Goal
Reframe the chosen pain point as an aspirational user outcome — the "product goal" for this improvement.

> ❌ "Add a search filter" (a solution)
> ✅ "Help users find the right property without feeling overwhelmed" (an outcome)

### Step 6: Solutions
Ideate 3+ solutions for the pain point. Then select one:
- Evaluate on: feasibility, impact on the pain, differentiation, alignment with business goal
- Describe the winning solution in enough detail to be visualized

### Step 7: Risks
Name 2–3 real risks:
- Technical feasibility
- Adoption risk (will users actually use it?)
- Cannibalization of other metrics
- Regulatory / privacy risks

## Common Question Examples
- "Pick any product and tell me how you would improve it."
- "How would you improve Zillow/Spotify/LinkedIn/Uber?"
- "What would you change about [current product at your company]?"
- "Improve [product's] retention."

## Coaching Tips
- Framing the improvement in a business goal context is the key differentiator — it shows you think like an owner, not a feature factory
- The most common mistake is jumping straight to solutions without establishing the business context and user pain
- Creative solutions that show you understand *why* users have the pain are more impressive than obvious feature additions
- Risks signal maturity and build credibility with the interviewer
